# Session Note

## Executive Summary

The party completed [[npc-vakish-baharus|Vakish Baharus]]'s third barrow lead by finishing exploration of the [[location-third-vakish-mound|third Vakish mound / flooded ceremonial tomb]], uncovering [[location-thornswild|Thornswild]] lore, recovering a magical cloak, claiming the [[item-hero-belt-gold-and-unicorn-horn-buckle|Belt of the Thornswild]] for Gron, and triggering a chaotic tablet that briefly revealed a larger Barrow Mounds map with a castle beyond the fog. On the way back to [[location-helix|Helix]], they investigated a lone light moving into the mounds and found a fresh serpent-and-skull symbol on a smaller mound, very likely tied to the half-orc church-worker / [[faction-steel-bone-brotherhood|Steel Bone Brotherhood]] hook but still unconfirmed in-world. Back in Helix, they sold grave goods to Vakish, learned the name [[location-castle-zentolin|Castle Zentolin]], retrieved the owl from Mazah, and had [[pc-sab|Sab]]'s cloak identified as a [[item-cloak-of-elvenkind|Cloak of Elvenkind]].

## What Happened

The session opened with inventory and continuity cleanup from Session 11. The party confirmed they were still carrying the [[item-gold-jackal-statue|second gold jackal]] for Vakish, had not yet returned to [[location-helix|Helix]], and were in the third of [[npc-vakish-baharus|Vakish Baharus]]'s three lead mounds. The [[item-charm-person-spell-scroll|Charm Person scroll]] found earlier was assigned to [[pc-gradrick|Gradrick]]. The party also confirmed that the owl magic item was still at Mazah's tower in Helix.

Inside the flooded ceremonial tomb, the party stood in the aftermath of the animated lizardfolk-statue fight. Gron was wearing the gold unicorn-horn inlaid belt, which granted a +4 bonus to strength-related things. The darkened elven statue now looked more like part of a raided tomb than an intact ceremonial scene.

[[pc-sab|Sab]] inspected a swollen, ironbound wooden door and judged it not trapped. [[pc-orlin|Orlin]] forced it open with bear-like hands. Beyond was a quieter burial chamber with a stone slab/catafalque, a green burial shroud, and faded wall paintings showing a hunter beneath a throned tree, lizardfolk raiders, and a wounded elven champion defending others. [[pc-gradrick|Gradrick]], with help from Orlin and Sylvan language knowledge, recovered the word "[[location-thornswild|Thornswild]]" from the faded writing and inferred that the buried figure was an elven warrior or champion from a place not too distant.

[[pc-sab|Sab]] approached the burial respectfully and performed a funerary prayer or chant. After the prayer, the shroud seemed more whole than it had before and took on the colors of nearby stone, moss, or black water. Sab took it as a cloak. The party understood it would help with hiding and might allow invisibility once properly understood.

[[pc-oogie|Oogie]] then used Trance on Gron. This granted Gron a luck token and gave both of them a vision of a nearby chamber containing a pedestal, a tablet, and an ornate urn or display vessel. Following the vision, the party forced open another swollen door and found that chamber. Oogie approached the pedestal carefully. The urn was valuable and finely made, but the tablet stood out more strongly: it gave off a chaotic aura. When Oogie picked up and read the tablet, it turned to ash and left a paper map in his hand. The map briefly showed the broader Barrow Mounds and a castle beyond the explored area, then faded blank. Oogie began trying to redraw it from memory, with Gron helping because he had also glimpsed it.

As Gron entered the chamber, he triggered a floor trap. The stone flipped away under the water, dropping him into a spike pit deep enough to submerge him. He took four damage, climbed back out, and warned the others. [[pc-sab|Sab]] then healed him with Cure Wounds. The party also inspected and took the ornate urn/amphora, which contained only ash or dust but looked valuable. They chose not to explore the deep water to the north.

The party exited the mound near evening. Other adventuring lights were heading back toward [[location-helix|Helix]], but one light was moving into the mounds near/past the obelisk. The group chose to observe rather than immediately return. They followed at a cautious distance and saw a large, broad-shouldered, hunched figure, possibly a half-orc, enter a smaller mound. This connected to an existing hook about a half-orc working at the church who may have a [[faction-steel-bone-brotherhood|Steel Bone Brotherhood]] connection.

The party approached the smaller mound stealthily but did not enter. The mound looked recently disturbed, with bent grass and a path showing repeated traffic. Light, incense, or candle warmth came from behind the door. The door had been freshly painted with a black serpent curling around a skull. The party decided they had enough information for the night and did not want to provoke a fight, especially if this led to the [[faction-steel-bone-brotherhood|Steel Bone Brotherhood]]. Gron proposed returning in daylight with the owl, hiding it near the mound, and using it to watch who comes and goes.

The party returned to [[location-helix|Helix]] safely at night and stayed at the [[location-brazen-strumpet|Brazen Strumpet]]. [[npc-bolo|Bolo]] recognized them and offered leftover stew; [[npc-tacey|Tacey]] was cleaning up. The party rested and received three XP each. The next morning they went first to the [[location-rose-quartz|Rose Quartz]] to unload treasure.

At the [[location-rose-quartz|Rose Quartz]], the party delivered the matching gold jackal to [[npc-vakish-baharus|Vakish Baharus]]. They sold the [[item-two-silver-diamond-rings|two silver rings with diamonds]] for 80 gp total and the ornate urn for 60 gp. They presented the belt from the tomb. Vakish recognized it as the [[item-hero-belt-gold-and-unicorn-horn-buckle|Belt of the Thornswild]] and asked what they had learned of its story. Based on the frescoes and statues, the party described an elven hero defending fleeing people from lizardfolk raiders. Vakish explained that elven tribes once lived north and east in a great forest called the [[location-thornswild|Thornswild]], but they had not been seen for a hundred years or more.

Vakish then produced a larger map and named the castle from [[pc-oogie|Oogie]]'s vision as [[location-castle-zentolin|Castle Zentolin]]. He said it sits on a crystal-clear lake and has not been seen for many decades because the fog blocks access. The party asked about the surrounding map, including fog-shrouded regions, a lost farmland/city, the southern desert/pyramid/monument, Mitra's Fist, and other routes. Vakish said the fog arrived roughly fifty or sixty years ago, after the farm folk fell and contact with the [[location-thornswild|Thornswild]] was lost. Traders who tried to continue through the fog never returned. The fog now seems to be easing day by day. The players expressed strong interest in pursuing Castle Zentolin next.

Vakish offered a choice: the party could keep the [[item-hero-belt-gold-and-unicorn-horn-buckle|Belt of the Thornswild]] or keep the protective cloak he had previously given them. They chose the belt, and [[pc-sab|Sab]] returned Vakish's cloak. The sale proceeds from the rings and urn totaled 140 gp, split five ways as 28 gp each, then reduced by 1 gp each for room and board. The party resupplied with oil, torches, spears, and related gear. Sab transferred the magical [[item-returning-dagger|returning thrown dagger]] to [[pc-oogie|Oogie]] because Oogie wanted better combat options.

Finally, the party went to Mazah's tower to retrieve the owl. The tower door had multiple "do not disturb" warnings and an illusory Mazah repeating the warning. The party disturbed him anyway. Mazah returned the owl and identified [[pc-sab|Sab]]'s recovered cloak as a [[item-cloak-of-elvenkind|Cloak of Elvenkind]]: it always helps the wearer hide, and sometimes, with focus, can turn the wearer completely invisible. The session ended with the party planning to use the owl to spy on the suspicious mound and with growing interest in traveling toward [[location-castle-zentolin|Castle Zentolin]].
