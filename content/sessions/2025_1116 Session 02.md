# Session Note

## Executive Summary

The session began in a lethal dungeon run launched from [[npc-locked-and-loaded|Locked and Loaded]]’s tower, where the party worked from a partial recap of prior exploration and continued testing the strange “[[item-archie-ticket|Archie ticket]]” resurrection mechanic. In the dungeon, they recovered a recently dead party member through the ticket system, experimented with offerings at a bovine/female-minotaur goddess statue, managed light and supplies carefully, used salt and turn-undead tactics against splitting undead blob creatures, and escaped with several valuables including a giant gold disc/coin, a], a [[item-very-good-greataxe|very good greataxe]], and an [[item-adamantine-bar|adamantine bar]]. The party then passed through a portal into a temple-centered settlement, spent the night under watchful but unsettling hospitality, and in the morning began orienting themselves, shopping, and negotiating with local NPCs including [[npc-osric|Osric]] and the armorer [[npc-celestia|Celestia]]. By session end, they had accepted what appears to be a job related to acquiring a [[item-silver-goblet|Silver Goblet]], and they had identified [[location-helix|Helix]] as a nearby destination on the map.

## What Happened

The group opened with table recap and continuity checks from the prior delve. They confirmed they had entered the dungeon from [[npc-locked-and-loaded|Locked and Loaded]]’s tower, had been issued “[[item-archie-ticket|Archie ticket]]s,” and believed those tickets function as some kind of resurrection or re-entry safeguard, though the exact rules were still unclear. [T227-257]

They also recapped prior discoveries: a dead family or group whose remains were associated with a projection device/orb, a circle of salt, and hostile undead blob creatures that split off smaller attackers when struck. The party strongly suspected salt was protective against those creatures. [T264-276]

In active play, the party recovered [[pc-orlin|Orlin]] after his earlier death. He revived naked via the ticket system and had to return to recover his equipment, apparently from the place where he died. This seems to confirm that death in this dungeon is not necessarily final if the ticket is used, but the exact destination or cost remains uncertain. [T456-512]

The group then interacted with a temple or shrine centered on a bovine or female-minotaur figure/statue. They debated proper offerings, placed a pole in the offering bowl, donated coins, and eventually poured oil into the brazier and lit it. A giant gold disc/coin associated with the shrine became available loot, though the divine response and exact logic of the shrine remained unclear. [T518-699]

While exploring further, the party stayed highly focused on torch economy, movement order, and escape planning. They identified an [[item-adamantine-bar|adamantine bar]] as valuable loot and discussed a room with a portal as their best exit strategy if the larger monster returned. They also reused known counters: turn undead had previously driven one creature away, and salt lines were placed at doorways to contain or deter the blob-like undead. They recovered caltrops and considered, but did not commit to, returning later to try killing the monster with salt. [T710-989]

The party ultimately went through the portal and emerged in moonlight at a temple district or settlement, apparently having crossed from the dungeon into a civilized location tied to a “mother” or temple authority. They were welcomed by temple attendants or residents, but the reception felt evasive and unnervingly controlled. The group requested baths and rest rather than pressing for answers immediately. [T1145-1259]

Overnight, they secured their room as best they could, remained suspicious of their hosts, and discussed their abilities, including belated realization that one character had a light spell that might have reduced their torch problem. [T1252-1399]

In the morning, the group got breakfast, asked basic orientation questions, and learned enough to place themselves in or near a Temple District. They bought food, talked with a map- or curio-related shop, and began splitting up for town actions. [T1607-1811]

They then dealt with two notable local merchants. [[npc-osric|Osric]], a curio dealer, negotiated aggressively over at least one item and mentioned a possible magic lead, but the party did not trust him much. The armorer [[npc-celestia|Celestia]] was presented as an alternative contact, and the party preferred her enough to hear her out. She appears to have bought the giant gold coin for 90 gold, and then offered the party 100 gold to obtain a [[item-silver-goblet|Silver Goblet]] from Osric, presumably letting them keep any margin they could negotiate. The party agreed. [T2009-2140]

At the close, the party discussed loot/XP accounting, recognized they had the gold coin, [[item-adamantine-bar|adamantine bar]], greataxe, and] as meaningful haul, and oriented themselves on a local map enough to identify [[location-helix|Helix]] as only a few hexes away. They also noted that the adamantine bar might be useful for a custom shield or weapon later. [T2204-2259]
