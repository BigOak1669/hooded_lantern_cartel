## Executive Summary

The party spent the session in Helix following the serpent-and-skull marked mound lead. Through the planted owl, Gradrick watched a courier called Mouse deliver a sack to a large half-orc-like figure inside the mound. Mazzah identified Oogie's cobra-headed staff, connected the painted sigil to an evil serpent-and-death cult, and took an old dark-arts spellbook for safekeeping in exchange for two useful spellbooks. ## What Happened

The session opened as the party returned to Helix after the broken-sigil mound expedition. Gradrick remained attuned to the owl hidden opposite the serpent-and-skull marked mound. After several quiet hours, he saw a skinny courier in greasy leathers and a low skullcap approach with a sack. The courier tapped a side stone twice, paused, then tapped once more. The door opened just enough for a large gray-green hand and shoulder to receive the sack. Gradrick could not hear the exchange but believed he could recognize the courier again.

Mox, the party's young torchbearer from the previous expedition, admitted finding three gold pieces in the rubble and asked to keep them in exchange for silence about the party's haul. The party allowed him to leave with the money after intimidating him into secrecy.

The party brought Mazzah the large spellbook recovered from the evil wizard's tomb. Gradrick established that it was mostly written in Common with some Diabolic material and contained at least an Eye Bite-like spell. Mazzah attributed it to a dark-arts practitioner whose name was transcribed inconsistently, warned that attempting its magic would be dangerous, and kept it for safekeeping. In exchange he provided two smaller spellbooks: Blind/Deafen for Sab and Hypnotize for Gradrick.

Mazzah identified Oogie's cobra-headed staff as a magical Staff of the Cobra: a +1 staff that can become a large serpent servant once per day. Snakes also regard Oogie as friendly. Oogie sold Mazzah the chipped red gemstone from the prior expedition for twenty gold pieces.

When shown the mound's painted sigil, Mazzah found similar imagery tied to an evil deity associated with serpents, death, and the underworld. The deity's exact name is unclear in the transcript. He considered the connection deeper and more organized than the Steel Bone Brotherhood activity the party had previously seen, but did not establish the Brotherhood's direct involvement.

At the Brazen Strumpet, Bolo recognized Gradrick's description of the courier as Mouse, a nearly or completely deaf local who communicates mainly through signs. Mouse was dining with a larger companion called Dennis and spending far more freely than usual. Bolo said Mouse had bought bread ends, onion tops, and sour ale earlier that day.

Sab reported the surveillance findings to Gant at the Fair Church. Gant said Brother Mort had been missing for two days and that one corpse was also missing, with the church believing Mort took it. The church wanted Mort gone and offered no reward for further intervention.

Orlin approached Mouse and Dennis at their table and learned that Mouse had a well-paid job delivering food to a large person in a Barrow Mounds cave. After the pair left the tavern, the party surrounded and questioned them. Dennis said they did not know the recipient's name, that Mouse delivered bread and drink twice daily, and that the mound smelled like a bad butcher's shop. The party paid one gold piece toward an extra delivery and threatened them against betrayal.

The party considered buying a sleep potion from Mazzah but rejected his price of one hundred gold. They also declined a fifty-gold Speak with Dead scroll. Instead, Bolo agreed to strengthen the sour ale Mouse would buy the next morning with an extremely potent clear spirit. The party planned to use the delivery before returning to the mound and recovering the owl.
