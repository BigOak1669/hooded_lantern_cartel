# Session Note

<div class="quick-recap-label">Quick Read</div>

## Executive Summary

The party used the fortified ale delivery to enter the serpent-and-skull marked mound. Oogie's Staff of the Cobra bypassed the painted serpent ward, and the party met Nara, an undead maid, in the entrance hall. They investigated a warm, movable altar and then entered the western workroom, where they found the missing Mort beside Mara's corpse and a gaunt ritualist named Tornar. Tornar animated Mara and other prepared dead, but Sab's Turn Undead ended the undead threat, destroying Nara and neutralizing the newly risen bodies. Orlin killed Tornar in bear form, Gradrick took Tornar's finger-bone wand, and Mort survived in grief. The party searched the workroom, recovered several unidentified objects and valuables, and found a damaged writ naming the Hollow Gate of Quasqueton. The mound, altar, Mort's future, and several objects remain unresolved.

## What Happened

The party began rested at the Brazen Strumpet, paid for room and board, and confirmed their plan to use Bolo's fortified sour ale. Mouse and Dennis delivered the parcel to the mound using the two-tap, pause, one-tap signal. The door opened only far enough for a green-gray hand to take the delivery. The party waited long enough for the recipient to drink before approaching.

Fog lay across the Barrow Mounds, and fresh black paint joined a serpent and iron skull across the stone door. Grond and Orlin approached first. Oogie accidentally touched the door while trying to get into position, causing the painted serpent to animate and slide from the skull. Because Oogie carried the Staff of the Cobra, the serpent treated him as friendly, curled around him, and disappeared into the fog without attacking.

Inside, the party found a dim, foul-smelling entrance hall and a half-decomposed woman sweeping. Her apron bore the name Nara, and an iron key ring hung at her waist. The group initially used hand signals, then Oogie released his rapport spores so the party could communicate directly. Nara responded as a household servant, asked them to wipe their boots, and allowed the party to take her keys. Sab examined the north altar: an iron serpent passed through a white stone skull, fresh black paint covered older household carvings, the stone was warm, and scratches around its base showed that it could move. The party did not open it.

The party heard an argument behind a western door and saw a chalk pentagram, candles, an open coffin, wrapped bodies, and a gaunt black-robed figure holding a wand shaped like an oversized finger bone. The door was unlocked. Inside, Mort knelt beside the wrapped body of Mara, and Tornar greeted the intruders without immediately attacking. Mort was flushed and unsteady from the fortified ale. He said Tornar had promised to bring Mara back.

Tornar began the rite. Necrotic energy flowed from the finger-bone wand into the coffin and wrapped bodies. Tornar coughed black ichor and collapsed to a knee as one corpse crawled free and the others began to stir. Orlin shot the crawling undead. Grond brought his holy-weapon-enhanced hammer down on another moving wrapped body. The risen undead attacked; one bit and grappled Orlin for five damage.

Sab then used Turn Undead. The newly risen dead ceased moving, and Nara turned to ash. Mort was left grieving beside the vanished remains of Mara. Gradrick pulled the wand from Tornar's hands. Tornar remained alive long enough to cough and protest that the bargain had been fulfilled, but Orlin transformed into a bear and tore him apart. Sab healed Orlin afterward, and Grond stayed with Mort while he broke down in grief.

The party searched the western rooms. They found journals and incoherent scraps referring to a serpent, a tablet as an inheritance rather than treasure, and a devouring ram; the names and meaning were not clear enough to settle. Oogie found a wooden tongue carving, a leather cord strung with old claws and bone beads, clothing, a fine green silk garment, a spell-scroll tube, and a chest containing eight gold pieces and twenty-eight silver pieces. The chest was packed into the party's bag of holding. Orlin found and pocketed a blue liquid with cloud-like forms floating inside it. Sab found a wax-sealed leather tube containing a damaged parchment writ granting a lawful bearer custody of the Hollow Gate of Quasqueton, subject to Rogahn the Fearless, Zelligar the Unknown, or a lawful heir. The party also recovered three silver bars, a dull dagger, and gold earrings.

The session ended with Mort still alive and with the party, the altar still unopened, and at least one other part of the mound unexplored. The table awarded eleven experience points; individual level and carry-over bookkeeping was discussed but not fully reconciled in the transcript.
