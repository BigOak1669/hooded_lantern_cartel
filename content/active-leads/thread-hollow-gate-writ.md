# The Hollow Gate Writ

## Where It Stands

The party recovered a damaged legal-looking writ from Tornar's rooms. It names the Hollow Gate of Quasqueton and grants a lawful bearer custody of its road-house, outer wall, spring, cave-mouth, stable court, and surrounding grounds, subject to Rogahn the Fearless, Zelligar the Unknown, or a lawful heir.

## Why It Matters

The document could be a future destination, a property claim, a route clue, or a problem created by someone else's competing rights. It is the first direct party-held evidence connecting those names to a concrete site rather than a legend or map reference.

## What Remains Uncertain

- The writ's legal force and the identity of its lawful bearer.
- The present location and condition of Quasqueton.
- Whether Quasqueton is the same place as Castle Zentolin, Castle Xyntillan, or another site.
- Whether Rogahn, Zelligar, or an heir still has a claim.
