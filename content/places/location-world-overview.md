# Location: World Overview

## Summary

- High-level campaign region linking the older Ore and Furnace arc to the current Helix-and-Barrow-Mounds expedition loop.

## Description

- Sessions 02-07 describe a local campaign corridor rather than a full atlas: the party emerges from older dungeon and temple-centered material near Ore and Furnace, travels the road to Helix, and keeps pushing from Helix into the Barrow Mounds.
- Helix now functions as the social and logistical center, while the Barrow Mounds are the main active danger zone.
- Travel, rumor flow, loot, injuries, and faction pressure keep cycling between the mounds and town, with the Ore and Furnace road still anchoring the older arc to the newer one.
- The Valley of Ruin regional map is the campaign's regional reference; only the player-safe version belongs on the public site.

## Current Relevance

- Serves as the top-level orientation note for the current regional geography and for the future nested location tree.
- Clarifies how the older Ore and Furnace material and the current Helix/Barrow focus fit together without pretending the whole wider world is already mapped.

## What The Players Know

- In Session 02, Helix appeared on the local map as the next meaningful destination after the earlier dungeon and temple-settlement events.
- In Session 03, the party physically traveled from Ore and Furnace toward Helix, making that road the bridge into the barrow-focused campaign.
- In Sessions 03-07, Helix became the repeating hub for lodging, rumors, hiring, magical help, religious help, loot sales, and recovery.
- In Sessions 04-07, the Barrow Mounds became the main expedition region driving most current treasure leads, deaths, and faction pressure.
