# Location: Ore and Furnace

## Summary

- Older settlement hub anchoring the campaign before the move to Helix: source of the portal-linked temple district, the party's loot sale, and Celestia's chalice job.

## Description

- Session 02 grounds this area as the broader settlement context around [[location-locked-and-loaded-tower|Locked and Loaded Tower]], the connected [[location-dungeon-underworld-complex|dungeon / underworld complex]], and the portal-linked [[location-temple-district-temple-settlement|Temple District / temple settlement]] where the party resurfaced.
- By the next-session recap, that broader settlement is named [[location-ore-and-furnace|Ore and Furnace]], tying together the earlier dungeon escape, the temple-side hospitality, the market shopping scene, and the party's deal with [[npc-celestia|Celestia]].
- The city now sits mostly off-screen, but it remains the clear origin point for the current Helix-and-chalice arc.
- Wade confirmed the regional map label as Ore and Furnace on 2026-05-30; avoid transcript drift such as "Ore Infernus."

## Current Relevance

- Ore and Furnace is the party's last established home-base before Helix and the source of the still-relevant chalice job, prior loot sale, and unfinished silver-dagger errand.

## What The Players Know

- The party entered this city-context after launching from [[location-locked-and-loaded-tower|Locked and Loaded Tower]], surviving the underworld complex, and escaping through the [[location-portal-chamber|portal chamber]].
- The settlement includes a temple-centered hospitality zone and a shopping / merchant scene where the party dealt with [[npc-celestia|Celestia]] and Osric.
- [[npc-celestia|Celestia]] bought loot here and hired the party to obtain the silver goblet / chalice that drove them toward [[location-helix|Helix]].
- The [[faction-mothers-of-the-hinge|Mothers of the Hinge]] are part of the city's recap-level continuity, but the exact relationship between their authority, the temple district, and the wider city layout is still a little loose.
- The GM-labeled regional map calls it City of Ore & Furnace.
