# NPC: Steel Bone cultist

## Summary

- Lowest visible field operatives of the [[faction-steel-bone-brotherhood|Steel Bone Brotherhood]], appearing as red-robed, steel-bone-masked cultists who shadowed the party around the Barrow Mounds and the Helix retreat route.

## Current Status

- Still active and unresolved. The party has seen them more than once, but every close encounter has left open whether they were real cultists, illusions, projections, or a mix of those.

## Known Facts

- The party first saw a distant red-robed figure at the edge of the mist near an already-looted mound.
- One of the figures returned [[pc-orlin|Orlin]]'s wave and moved toward the prominent barrow.
- Similar red-robed, steel-bone-masked figures appeared again during the retreat from the mounds in Session 07.
- The strongest current read is that these are low-ranking Brotherhood cultists or watchers rather than independent NPCs.

## What The Players Know

- These sightings recur across multiple sessions and are not just a one-off omen.
- Their behavior suggests surveillance, intimidation, guidance, or bait.
- The party treats them as part of the Brotherhood threat cluster around the obelisk-area barrows.

## Relationships

- Field-level operatives of the [[faction-steel-bone-brotherhood|Steel Bone Brotherhood]].
- Notably interacted with [[pc-orlin|Orlin]] by returning his wave.
- Closely associated with the [[location-prominent-barrow-with-standing-stones-and-shaft|prominent barrow]], the obelisk-area mounds, and the party's growing sense that the Brotherhood is actively monitoring expeditions.

## Session History

- 2026-02-22 [[2026_0222 Session 06]] — Observed twice in the mist, responded to Orlin's wave, and seemed to draw the party toward the prominent barrow.
- 2026-03-08 [[2026_0308 Session 07]] — Reappeared as red-robed, steel-bone-masked figures during the retreat route back toward Helix.

## Notes / Uncertainty

- It remains unclear whether the party has seen one recurring cultist, several interchangeable low-ranking cultists, or projected images used by the Brotherhood.
- Their exact motive is unknown: warning, luring, intimidation, surveillance, or ritual boundary-keeping all remain plausible.
