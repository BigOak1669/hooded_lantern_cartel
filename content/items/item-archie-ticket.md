# Item: Archie ticket

## Summary

- A death-return ticket tied to Archie, used to restore a dead companion through a still only partly understood ritual or contract.

## Mechanical Details

- Earlier evidence indicates the ticket can be torn in half to facilitate a teammate's return.
- Session 07 confirms that tearing an [[item-archie-ticket|Archie ticket]] successfully restored [[pc-oogie|Oogie]] at the [[location-fair-church|Fair Church]].
- Oogie retained half a ticket afterward, suggesting the ticket may persist in fractional form rather than being simply consumed all at once.
- Prior lore frames the ticket as a contractual arrangement involving fractions of the user's soul, but the exact rules, limits, and consequences are still unresolved.

## Known Facts

- Archie tickets can reverse or bypass death under at least some conditions.
- They are tied to a broader liminal process involving post-death transition rather than simple mundane healing.
- Session 07 connected the ticket directly to a devil-managed interstitial encounter involving Oogie and [[pc-dern|Dern]].
- The party has now seen a successful live use rather than just hearing theory about the item.

## What The Players Know

- Tearing the ticket can restore a dead companion.
- The process may leave part of the ticket still in circulation afterward.
- The system appears contractual and potentially soul-linked.
- The full rules, costs, and edge cases remain unclear.

## Session History

- 2025-11-16 [[2025_1116 Session 02]] — Archie tickets were introduced as a resurrection-linked party asset with warnings about soul fractions and repeat use.
- 2026-03-08 [[2026_0308 Session 07]] — The party tore Oogie's Archie ticket at the Fair Church to restore him, and Oogie retained half a ticket afterward.

## Notes / Uncertainty

- It is still unclear whether the bright-room/devil experience is always part of ticket use or only part of Oogie's case.
- The exact costs of repeated tearing, subdivision, or reuse remain unresolved.
- Dern's entry into play through the same death-return logic suggests the tickets may be embedded in a broader system than the party currently understands.
